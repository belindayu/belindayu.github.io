Why I want to learn front-end development

I have always thought it was interesting to learn about why we perceive certain formats/structures/colors more appealing than others in addition to the complexity and design process that goes into creating the seemingly ordinary things we use everyday.

I remember that I first learned about this, and still do, from reading articles about UI/UX on Medium.

I knew I liked seeing the visual side of projects, whether it was a flyer for a club event or presentation slides for a class, so that it was user-friendly and visually pleasing.

So, I took this as a sign that front-end development would be a great path for me. During my winter break, I finally took a leap on a HTML/CSS course and love it! I now view every website I go on differently, knowing that someone thoughtfully designed its layout and components for a reason.

I think it's pretty cool that I can build an entire website from scratch that many people will see and use. I'm eager to apply my knowledge and learn about how it all works!
