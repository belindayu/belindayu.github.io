Why I decided to get into front-end development:

I have always thought it was interesting to learn about why we perceive certain formats/structures/colors more appealing than others in addition to the complexity and design process that goes into building the seemingly ordinary things we use everyday.

I remember that I first learned about some of this from reading various articles on UI/UX on Medium (one of my favorite sites!).

I knew I liked seeing the visual side of coding and designing, whether it was a website or a flyer for a club event, so that it was visually pleasing and user-friendly.

So, I took this as a sign that front-end development may be a good path for me and finally took a leap on a HTML/CSS course! I now view every website I go on differently, knowing that it had to be built from scratch. I'm eager to learn how to build a responsive and useful website for others to use!
